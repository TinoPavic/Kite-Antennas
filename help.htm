Kites can be very effective lifters for wire antennas.

Lift is vertical force, and drag is horizontal force.

Bigger kites have larger area, and produce bigger forces.

Forces increase with square of wind speed.

Kites for strong wind must be strongly built and heavier.

Suitable kite types are:

1. Sled - very steady lifter for medium to strong winds.

2. French Military Kite - great for medium to very strong winds.

3. Box Kites

4. Winged boxes

4. Parafoils

Controls Explained

F = Line tension in kg, without any payload.

a = Elevation angle in degrees, without any payload.

Lift and Drag are calculated from tension and elevation.

m = Payload mass

h = payload height

g = Loaded elevation angles in degrees

d = Kite anchor distance in metres

Hookup wire 1mm diameter weights about 1 kg for 80 m length.

This is enough wire to make half wave vertical for 160 M band.

Enamelled Copper Wire (ECW) 0.8 mm diameter weighs 0.3 kg for 80 m length
